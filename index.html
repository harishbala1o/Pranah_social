Our Story

Pranah’s story began when I met a girl named Dimpy Das, who was a stranger to me and who died in 2015 after a long battle with cancer when she was only 22 years old. It was her who inspired me to devote my spare time to discover whether there were any cancer prevention methods. We've all met cancer patients who are battling the disease in various ways. But we remember those who have a fighting spirit and keep a smile through it all the more. Dimpy Das was one of these people.

Every day while she battled cancer presented new challenges, a new battle with the side effects of that fatal illness, and Dimpy's resilience shone through in the midst of it all. Dimpy's tenacity and perseverance inspired me to delve deeper into the various ways in which this disease can be combated, and I carried thorough research.

Because of her, I discovered a plant called Lakshmi Taru, which was researched and promoted by Dr Shyamsundar Joshi and Dr Shantha Joshi, both botanists who have been researching this plant for 30 years and treating cancer patients 365 days a year at their residence in RT Nagar, Bengaluru.

Pay attention. Be astonished. Tell about it.

And that was the watershed moment in my life. Reading about the plant and speaking with cancer patients who used this leaf powder convinced them, and later me, of its medicinal value. As a result, I've begun planting these trees throughout India, spreading the storey of traditional and complementary medicine and its role in immunotherapy. That was the beginning of this initiative, and the motivation for the formation of Pranah Social Association in December 2019. It is, above all a tribute to a friend.

Since then, we have been inspiring people all over India to plant these trees in order to create a culture of healing awareness in the community.

-Preethi Potupureddy
